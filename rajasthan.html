About Jaipur

Jaipur is also known as pink city and has many places to visit. Some famous places are Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal, Jal Mahal, Jantar Mantar, Jaigarh and Nahargarh fort. Every place has its own unique expierince which cannot be described in words and can only be felt. The joy becomes double when one visits the place with his family or friends. The ambience of the place makes the experience even more mesmerizing. One gets to learn about the glorious history of India. It tells them how diverse India is. This makes the experience unforgettable and this is what attracts people to visit these monuments in Jaipur. Jaipur is a wonderland of culture and heritage, brimming with architectural gems. Also, Jaipur is a prominent stop in India's famous Golden Triangle.



About monuments

Amber Fort: The fort was build by Raja Man Singh. It represents Rajput architectural designs. The place was resided by the Rajput Maharajas and their families.

Hawa Mahal: Palace of Winds is a five storey house constructed for the royal family members of the Maharaja. It resembles a bee-hive, some say it is like the Crown of Lord Krishna.

Jal Mahal: Take an evening walk as the sun kisses the pink sandstone walls of Jal Mahal standing in the blue waters of the Man Sagar Lake and a great place for birds.

Nahargarh Fort: Nahargarh Fort is known as a haunted place by many people and has a historic significance. There is a rooftop hotel from where you get a mesmerizing view of the city of Jaipur.



Positive Impacts of Increased Tourism

The increase in tourism boosts the economy. As more people visit our city, the revenue increases exponentially. This is very profitable to local restaurants, shopping centers and stores. Tourism creates a cultural exchange between tourists and local citizens. Exhibitions, conferences, and events usually attract foreigners.Furthermore,foreign tourists bring diversity and cultural enrichment to the hosting country. Tourism also increases the job opportunities for people. There are endless job opportunities for the youth in travel agencies, tour operators, ticketing officers, transport officers, logistics, hotel management, airlines, and cruises.



Negative Impacts of Increased Tourism

Tourism leads to enviornmental pollution. Greenhouse gas emission from air travel and littering at popular locations are the major problems.Due to the increasement in population, resources become unsustainable and get exhausted. At hotspot tourist places, crime rates are very high, usage of alcohol and illegal drugs also increase. It also forces local residents to leave their home and displace because of very high noise and crowdedness.Road accidents is another negative outcome of tourism development since visitors are not aware of local rules, driving norms, and road conditions.
